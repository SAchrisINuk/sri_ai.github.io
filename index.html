How your child learns about AI at SRI ◆

Artificial intelligence is part of your child's world. We teach it in an age-appropriate, carefully supervised way so pupils grow into safe, confident and thoughtful users. Here is exactly what that looks like, year by year.

Age-appropriate by design

Independent generative AI is only used from Year 9, when pupils are aged 13 and over. Younger children explore AI through play and structured tasks.

Always teacher-led

Children meet AI through weekly Computing and Trailblazer Technology lessons, guided by their teachers at every step.

Guard-railed tools only

We use approved, school-managed platforms such as Umety, Canva and managed Copilot. No open, unsupervised access.

Safety and ethics first

Pupils learn to protect their data, check accuracy, spot bias and question what AI tells them.

The learning journey

AI learning grows gradually with your child. It begins with the foundations of clear thinking and builds, across the school, towards readiness for supervised generative AI from Year 9.

The age and supervision model

The most important thing to understand is when generative AI (the kind that writes or creates) is used independently. That only happens from Year 9, when pupils are aged 13 and over. Throughout SRI, from FS1 to Year 8, pupils learn through teacher-led, guard-railed activities only.

Your questions answered

Will my child be left to use AI on their own?+
No. All AI learning is led by teachers through structured lessons. Independent use of generative AI does not happen below Year 9 (age 13 and over). Younger children experience AI through play, guided tasks and safe, guard-railed platforms.
My child is in the early years. Are they really "doing AI"?+
Not in the way adults use AI. In FS1 to Year 2, children build the thinking that AI depends on: spotting patterns, putting steps in order and giving simple instructions to robots like Bee-Bots. It is playful, hands-on and screen-light.
When does generative AI (like chatbots) come in?+
Independent use of generative AI is introduced only from Year 9, when pupils are aged 13 and over, in line with the requirement that these tools are for that age group. Throughout SRI, from FS1 to Year 8, pupils do not use generative AI independently. They experience only teacher-led, guard-railed activities that prepare them to use it safely later.
Is my child's personal data safe?+
Yes. We use school-managed, approved tools and teach pupils from an early age never to share personal information with AI. Protecting data is a core part of our responsible AI lessons.
How do I talk to my child about AI at home?+
Ask them what they made or programmed this week, and encourage the same questions we use in class: Where did this information come from? Is it accurate? Could it be biased? Curiosity and healthy scepticism are exactly what we are building.
